Build a static browser game called “Food Engines” for the Summer into AI Week 2: Independence Engines competition.

Concept:
Food Engines is a bright, cartoon, American-themed farm-grid browser game about food independence. The intended broad reference point is old Facebook-era casual farm games like FarmVille: simple square crop plots, plant/wait/harvest loops, cute farm buildings, visible crop timers, request boards, expansion dopamine, and cozy clicky farming. However, this must be original. Do not copy FarmVille names, art, UI, sounds, monetization, crop assets, layouts, social mechanics, or branding.

The twist:
This is not just “grow tomatoes for coins.” The player is building the food engines underneath a small American town: soil, water, seeds, farm stands, school lunches, firehouse chili, diners, canning jars, root cellars, county fair pies, co-op deliveries, food pantry reserves, and emergency meals when the grocery truck is late.

Tone:
Cartoon Americana. Bright, funny, civic, farmstand, county fair, red barn, blue ribbon, diner coffee, parade fundraiser, school cafeteria, church-basement-casserole energy without becoming partisan or religiously preachy. It should feel like a cheerful Facebook farm game got invaded by a community food-security spreadsheet wearing overalls.

Important IP/style guard:

* Do not copy FarmVille, Stardew Valley, Hay Day, Farm Story, or any commercial game’s art, names, UI, sounds, mechanics one-for-one, characters, logos, crop assets, or monetization.
* Do not use real political party branding.
* Do not use real politician names or likenesses.
* Avoid copyrighted mascots, logos, or branded food names.
* Original cartoon farm art only, created with CSS/SVG/canvas/procedural shapes.

Technical requirements:

* Static browser app only.
* Use Vite + TypeScript.
* Canvas, SVG, or DOM grid is acceptable. Prefer simple DOM/SVG/canvas over a heavy engine unless already practical.
* No backend.
* No accounts.
* No analytics.
* No telemetry.
* No remote AI/model calls.
* Store current run and best scores in localStorage.
* Must pass:

  * npm ci
  * npm run typecheck
  * npm run build
  * npm audit --audit-level=moderate
* Do not push or deploy unless explicitly authorized.

Core theme:
Food independence is an engine. A farm is not just crops. It is soil, water, seeds, labor, storage, kitchens, preservation, markets, weather, animals, transportation, and community trust. The player builds those engines on a tiny cartoon farm grid and tries to feed a town without collapsing into lettuce regret.

Main game structure:

* One run lasts 21 days.
* The player has a small farm grid, roughly 10x8 or 12x10.
* The farm should look like a playful American country map:

  * red barn
  * dirt plots
  * white fence
  * roadside farm stand
  * water tower or well
  * silo
  * flag bunting
  * county road edge
  * diner sign or town board
  * picnic table/checkered cloth accents
* Each day has:

  1. Morning weather and town bulletin.
  2. Town request board.
  3. Player actions: plant, water, harvest, build, preserve, sell/feed.
  4. End Day resolution.
* Goal: finish with high Food Security, Town Fed score, Pantry Reserves, Market Trust, Soil Health, and Farm Sustainability.

FarmVille-like casual loop, but original:

* Click an empty plot.
* Choose a crop.
* Crop appears as a cute icon/sprite/emoji-like tile.
* Crop grows through visible stages.
* Crop tile shows a tiny timer/day badge.
* Water droplet appears if thirsty.
* Sparkle or outline appears when ready to harvest.
* Harvest gives satisfying feedback: bounce, sparkle, small floating “+ Tomatoes” or “+ Fresh Food.”
* Town request board asks for crops/meals/pantry items.
* Completing requests gives money, trust, food security, seeds, or engine discounts.
* Missing requests creates funny local bulletin consequences.
* No real social network integration. Town Requests replace Facebook neighbor requests.

Run length:
21 days.

Action economy:

* Player gets 6 actions per day.
* Planting costs 1 action.
* Watering costs 1 action unless automated by rain/irrigation.
* Harvesting costs 1 action or can be free if needed for better feel. Choose one and document it in README.
* Building an engine costs 1 action and money.
* Feeding town or preserving food costs 1 action unless automated by an engine.
* End Day resolves growth, weather, spoilage, demand, and events.

Weather:
Each day has a forecast with clear effects.

Weather types:

* Sunny:
  Normal growth, dries soil.
* Rain:
  Free watering, fills rain barrels.
* Heat Wave:
  Extra water demand, lettuce suffers, spoilage rises.
* Cool Morning:
  Slower growth, less spoilage.
* Storm:
  Fills water, may damage crops, town demand may rise.
* Perfect Farm Day:
  Growth boost, Farm Joy boost.
* Dry Wind:
  Water drains, soil stress rises.
* County Fair Weather:
  Market Trust and fair-demand bonus.

Food engines/upgrades:
Implement at least eight total upgrades across categories. Each should be visually represented on the farm grid or side panel.

   Compost Bin:
   Converts spoilage/plant scraps into compost. Compost improves soil health.

Flavor: “Hole in ground. Civilization follows.”

   Harvests sometimes return seeds.

Protects a few tiles from weather.
Flavor: “A tiny glass republic for delicate plants.”

4. Kitchen Engine
   Soup Pot:
   Converts tomatoes, beans, potatoes, and corn into town meals.
   Flavor: “Hot civic glue.”

Bakery:
Converts wheat and apples into bread/pies.
Flavor: “Turns grain into morale.”

Multiplies Town Fed from mixed crops.
Flavor: “Where surplus becomes supper.”

5. Preservation Engine
   Cannery:
   Converts tomatoes/beans/corn into pantry food.
   Flavor: “Summer panic sealed in jars.”

Root Cellar:
Reduces spoilage and boosts potatoes/apples/pumpkins.
Flavor: “Underground confidence.”

Drying Rack:
Preserves lighter crops.
Flavor: “Low-tech snack diplomacy.”

6. Market Engine
   Roadside Farm Stand:
   Sells surplus fresh food for money.
   Flavor: “Honor-system capitalism with tomatoes.”

CSA Chalkboard:

Flavor: “Subscription vegetables, but neighborly.”

County Market Stall:
Better prices but raises demand pressure.
Flavor: “Now the town knows you have tomatoes.”

7. Animal Engine, optional if time allows

   Consumes corn or feed, produces eggs.

Goat Pen:
Consumes feed, produces milk.
Flavor: “Dairy chaos in a fence.”

Players plant crops, harvest food, build water/soil/seed/kitchen/preservation/market engines, and answer town requests for school lunches, diner pies, firehouse chili, pantry drives, and county fair meals.
